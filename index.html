About

Seeking under the hood and imagining with "What If" questions. Two facts describing me.

Robotics Researcher

I have a B.Sc. from Computer Engineering department of Middle East Technical University (METU). I am pursuing my M.Sc. from the same department. I've been studying robotics, computer vision and human-robot interaction at Kovan and ROMER for nearly 4 years.

  • Birthday: 1998   Age: 24
  • City: Ankara, Turkey
  • Working at: ROMER, METU
  • Degree: Computer Engineering Ms.C.

My greatest passion is revealing the algorithm behind evertyhing I wonder, and my way of understanding a stuff is feeling it with touching, seeing and showing it to others. Therefore, I observe to see patterns and states in anything. However, taking one more step, playing with the stuff enables the power of intervention instead of just observing. Consequently, if I have a chance to observe, I observe. However, if I see a way to interact with a stuff, I interact. For instance, if I see a robot, I move it and play with it's sensors. If I see a hoverboard, I drive (sorry Romer colleagues). Therefore, I spend most of my time with playing, i.e. trying and failing.

I guess that gives a clue why I've chosen robotics. Robotic gives me the opportunity of touching, seeing, showing and impressing people. On the other hand, computer vision is one way of robot's sensing of the world. Moreover, one can visualize nearly each step of process. This is like the trip of the image that we can observe. Thus, it attracts me.

Actually, there are other fields of computer science I wonder but could not find time to dive and drown in them. For instance, I studied motion planning and collision avoidance during my first intership. Another field that I would like to explore is computer graphics which engaged my attention at the computer graphics course.

When I am not at the lab, you can find me at playing football or computer games, doing exercises with weights, reading classics or trying to learn playing electric guitar.
